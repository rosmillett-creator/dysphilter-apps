About Hoa Wairua

Hoa Wairua means "soul friend" or "spiritual companion" in te reo Māori. It is the sister of Enef Car, the Cornish phrase for the same deep idea — a companion who walks beside you at the level of the soul.

This app does not diagnose, treat, or fix. It helps you offer steady, calming presence through your own hands — either resting gently on the body or hovering just above it. The energy flows through you. You are the channel, not the source.

Reiki means "universal life energy" in Japanese — Rei (universal) and Ki (life force). You do not need to be attuned, trained, or certified to use this app. Your intention to be present and your willingness to let energy flow is enough.

Hoa Wairua is for wherever you are: on a train, on a bus, in a car, on a plane. In a waiting room. At a desk. In a lesson. At someone's bedside in hospital or hospice. Before school, before work, before sleep. In the middle of the night when sleep won't come.

It is for a person soothing their own anxiety, a parent sharing calm with their child, a child teaching a parent or sibling, a carer holding someone's hands in a hospital room, a friend sitting beside someone who is ill. It can be used alone or together. A child can learn it, and a child can teach it.

No data collected. No account needed. Everything stays on your device.
