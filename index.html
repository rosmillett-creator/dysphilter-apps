Before You Begin

When you are giving treatment to someone else, what you carry with you becomes part of their experience. This is a gentle guide to help you create a calm, considerate space for the person you are treating.

🤲

Your Hands

Wash your hands before you begin. If you have hand cream, a light, unscented one is best. Your hands are the bridge between you and the person you are treating — make sure that bridge is clean and warm.

💨

Your Breath

Be aware of your breath. Coffee, food, smoking — these are things the person you are treating may notice when you are close to them. If you can, brush your teeth or have a mint before you begin. You do not need to say anything about it. Just be aware.

📍

Your Proximity

Do not lean over someone when you are giving them a treatment. Work from the side where you can. If they are in a hospital bed, respect the space around the bed. If they are lying down or sitting in a chair, position yourself so they can see you without straining. You want to be close enough to reach them comfortably, but not so close that your presence feels heavy.

👃

Your Scent

Be aware of body odour and strong perfumes. The person you are treating may be sensitive to smells, especially if they are unwell, in pain, or going through treatment. A neutral, clean presence is the most considerate choice. If you smoke, a quick wash and change of top can make a real difference.

🪑

Their Space

Whether they are in a hospital bed, lying on a sofa, or sitting in a chair — their space is theirs. Ask before you adjust pillows, move a table, or change anything around them. If they are in a hospital, be aware of wires, monitors, and medical equipment. Work around their world, not through it.

🌙

Your Presence

Calm is something you carry, not something you perform. Before you start, take a breath. Settle yourself. The person you are treating will feel whether you are rushed or grounded. Your state becomes part of their experience. A few seconds of quiet before you begin is not wasted time — it is the most important part.

About Enef Car

Enef Car means "soul friend" in Cornish (Kernewek). It is the sister of the Irish phrase Anam Cara — a companion who walks beside you at the level of the soul.

This app does not diagnose, treat, or fix. It helps you offer steady, calming presence through your own hands.

Hands, ears, face, and head are the heart of Enef Car — these areas are closest to the brain, so the calming signal travels fast and the effect is immediate. These four areas cover everything you need, for yourself or for someone you are caring for.

Enef Car is for wherever you are: on a train, on a bus, in a car, on a plane. In a waiting room. At a desk. In a lesson. At someone's bedside in hospital or hospice. Before school, before work, before sleep. In the middle of the night when sleep won't come.

It is for a person soothing their own anxiety, a parent sharing calm with their child, a child teaching a parent or sibling, a carer holding someone's hands in a hospital room, a friend sitting beside someone who is ill. It can be used alone or together. It can teach anyone — at any age — that their own hands are a tool for calm. A child can learn it, and a child can teach it. The skill lasts a lifetime.

No data collected. No account needed. Everything stays on your device.
